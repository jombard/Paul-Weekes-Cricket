Weekesy's Quick Tips

    Batting

  • Master the basics. Remember to watch the ball as closely as possible.
  • Instead of trying to hit every ball it is better to block the good balls so that you can be there to punish the bad balls. You can't score runs from the pavilion.
  • When running between the wickets run your first run hard and always be on the lookout to push for a second.
    		•

    Bowling

  • Keep it simple. 6 consistent balls in the same area is much better than trying a variation every ball. Make the batsman work to get runs.
  • The best bowlers in the world aim to hit the top of off every ball. This works for them and it will work for you.
    		•

    Fielding

  • Everyone spends more time fielding than any other skill in the game, so you might as well learn to enjoy it. If you enjoy it you will automatically be better at it.
  • Just like batting and bowling, you need to practice this skill. If you expect to take a catch in a game, practice taking as many catches as possible at training.
    		•

    Match Day

  • Pack your bag the night before a game so there is no rush in the morning.
  • Arrive early so you get a good spot in the changing room and get prepared.
  • Go out to the pitch and visualise the day's play.
  • Make a mental note of the boundary sizes and if there is a slope on the pitch.
  • Last of all get yourself warmed up and ready for the day's play.
  • Finally, go out there and enjoy yourself, show off your ability. Have fun!
